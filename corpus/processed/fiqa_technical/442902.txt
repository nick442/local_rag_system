We're not competing with ourselves when it comes to wages, we're competing with much lower living standards in other countries. The result is stunted wage growth due to a huge vacuum existing between the consumer markets of the Developed and Developing countries: Wage growth is being absorbed by differences in purchasing power parity, resulting in almost deflationary pressure. The effect on employment numbers means that unemployment can hit zero in one economy without inflationary pressure, because the slack can be picked up *in another economy*.
