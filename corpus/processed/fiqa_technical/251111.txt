It's not rocket science. Verizon, for example, runs a 6% margin. Finances of huge corporations are quite public. Sure, the telcos could invest more into wireless infrastructure, but it's not like these companies aren't operating at close to their capacity. And when you look down a rung on the ladder at tower companies, they're heavily leveraged and generally in debt due to expansion that they hope will pay off in the future. The way you see it discussed on reddit, you would think that we could have 100% 7g coverage if it wasn't for corporate greed, but in reality these companies are already running at very high speeds and make liberal use of leverage and debt to expand as quick as they can.
