"The few individuals who create the corporations that take advantage of the sharing economy, making it broadly accepted and utilized, make billions. Those who actually do the work of ""sharing"", not so much, unless they are able to do so out of excess of funds rather than through borrowing. Those who utilize the sharing economy save some money because they aren't making the investments in infrastructure (cars, housing, whatever) and so aren't paying interest or expending use value. They can invest that money in sharing economy corporations, gaining value. So the losers in the sharing economy are those who borrowing in order to ""share."""
