Summarized article: The Department of Commerce reported that retail sales increased 1.1% in September, exceeding analysts' expectations. The biggest jump in consumer spending came from car sales, gasoline and electronics. Electronic sales jumped 4.5%, likely due to the debut of Apple's iPhone 5. Additionally, higher gas prices led to 2.5% increase in spending at gas stations. The latest report suggests the economy is expanding as consumer spending drives about 2/3 of the US economy. Other data showed manufacturing activity in New York state shrank for the third straight month as US manufacturers feel the effects of a slowing global economy.
