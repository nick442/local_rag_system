It is a more subtle form of force to be destined to live as a second class citizen for no reason other than the station of your birth or your religious beliefs. Whether this status is codified in law or through the collective will of the ownership class, it is a far worse injustice than being forced to do business with someone you don't like. Equating racial justice with theft is just one example of why Libertarianism is not taken seriously as a political philosophy.
