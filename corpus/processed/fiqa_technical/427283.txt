FHA doesn't have PMI which stands for Private Mortgage Insurance. MI for Mortgage Insurance is the generic name. FHA has MMI or Mutual Mortgage Insurance. With PMI you can apply to have it removed if the value of your home increases in value. With MMI (from FHA) that is not possible. The current FHA rules are here 1. For your case, 85% LTV: the rule is no longer, PMI ends when you hit the 78% mark. It is you must pay 11 years PMI for a 30 mortgage with a 78% to 90% LTV. The current rules took effect June 3rd, 2013. 1. Note that many websites, including Wikipedia, have not been updated to reflect the current rules.
