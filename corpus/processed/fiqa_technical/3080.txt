That period was also significantly less regulated as measured by the size of the federal register. As of 2013, there is 18x as much regulation as in 1950 ([source](https://www.mercatus.org/video/visualizing-growth-federal-regulation-1950)). Additionally, the middle class didn't pay taxes in the 90% bracket and the rich most likely didn't either as the rich tend to receive the majority of income through capital gains or other passive income. The top capital gains rate was significantly less--25% starting in 1942--than the top rate for ordinary income during the post-war period. Edit: spacing
