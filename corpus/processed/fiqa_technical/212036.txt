1. Have enough funds to run the business and pay yourself the first year, plus 30% 2. Know your market, the good and the bad, then develop your niche. 3. Don't just sell merchandise. Sell service. Anyone can sell merchandise. 4. Listen to your customers, but don't follow their advice. Customers have agendas. 5. Set company and personal goals. If you can't meet both, you'll be miserable. 6. Plan and control your inventory (hold as little inventory as feasibly possible). 7. Market your business (understand the differences between Marketing, Advertising & Sales). 8. Keep it simple and focused (expect to work 60+ hrs a week for the first 2 to 3 years).
