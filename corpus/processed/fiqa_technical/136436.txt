> plus they get to be the Wal-Mart of the internet and outprice all of their competitor It's all [about market cap](http://evonomics.com/amazon-accounting-corporate-profits-rich-peoples-income-invisible-bezos/) not the appearance of investment. They turn most of their profits into dividends, so they get taxed (from investors) once at a 20% rate, rather than a 35% then 20% (48% total). They are basically exploiting a glitch where dividend payments are tax deductible to the company, rather than (rightfully) being considered profits being collected by the owners.
